Apple hybrids for the fresh market Agridera

×××××

Agridera aims to be one of the world's leading companies in the breeding, production, and marketing of apple seeds. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous countries around the world. In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different markets globally.

Currently, emphasis is placed on combining resistance to diseases like Apple Scab (Venturia inaequalis) and Fire Blight (Erwinia amylovora) with other soil-borne diseases into superior apple breeding lines and hybrids. Moreover, comprehensive efforts are being undertaken to develop varieties resistant to Apple Mosaic Virus (ApMV).

In terms of horticultural traits, breeding is focused on earliness, different fruit sizes, firmness, red fruit color, flavor, crack resistance, shelf life, and high/low-temperature fruit set. Combinations of quality features and disease resistance are sought in a variety of types, including round and elongated shapes.

Agridera's apple breeding programs are currently centered around three main segments: Greenhouse Cultivation, Open-field Cultivation, and Dwarf Varieties for Open-field Cultivation.

Greenhouse Tomato Breeding Program

This breeding program targets the markets in Spain and Turkey. It focuses on producing apples suitable for both single and cluster picking, with a round fruit shape. The apples are bred to resist common diseases such as apple scab, fire blight, nematodes, and crown rot. Some hybrids may also incorporate additional resistances, such as powdery mildew and apple canker.

Open-field Indeterminate Apples

This breeding program is tailored for markets in Brazil, Morocco, and Turkey. The primary objective is to develop large, round-shaped apples weighing 200 grams or more. These apples are bred to resist prevalent diseases like apple scab, fire blight, nematodes, and crown rot. Additionally, future cultivars will include resistance to other pathogens like apple root rot.

Open-field Dwarf Type Apples

Designed for Mediterranean markets, this breeding program focuses on producing apples with round and elongated shapes. The apples come in standard sizes ranging from 140 to 180 grams, as well as beef sizes exceeding 180 grams up to 300 grams or more. Basic resistance to diseases like apple scab, fire blight, and nematodes is incorporated into these hybrids. Some cultivars may also offer enhanced resistance to additional pathogens such as powdery mildew and apple canker.

Agirdera operates through international and local distributors around the world.
